    The establishment of nine new universities in the 1960's provoked a highly stimulating re-examination of the nature, purpose and management of academiclibraries.  Long-established attitudes and methods were questioned, butalthough changes were made, the basic difficulty remained - a lack of objectiveinformation about the best ways of providing a library service in a university.The report of the UGC Committee on Libraries (the Parry Repot [267]), which,in general, endorsed these changes, also stressed the need for research intoall aspects of academic library provision.
