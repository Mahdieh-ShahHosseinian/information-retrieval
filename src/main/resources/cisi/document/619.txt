   Initiated as an experiment, supported by the Division of MedicinalChemistry of Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, the operationalretrospective retrieval service offered by BIOSIS, now in its eighth year, exploits a file created essentially without vocabulary control.. The filecomprises some 40 million index access points to 1.87 million referencesin research biology announced in Biological Abstracts and BioResearchIndex since September 1959.. A pragmatic program of file building criteriahas been pursued, originally with modest support from the Office of ScienceInformation Service of the National Science Foundation, which has providedimproved retrieval and an annual summary of the vocabulary of the literatureavailable to anyone interested (1)..
