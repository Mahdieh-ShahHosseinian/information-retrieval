  This book reports the findings from a study of job motivationbased on a fresh approach to this problem.  It is an importantstudy, since the analyses and interpretations of the authorssuggest that a breakthrough may well have been made to providenew insights into the nature and method of operation of jobattitudes.  The senior authors were well prepared for their task, havingrecently completed a comprehensive review and analysis of theresearch in this field, reported in Job Attitudes: Review ofResearch and Opinion.  This review of several thousand articlesand books regarding the factors relating to job attitudes and theeffect of job attitudes on work performance indicated muchdisagreement and confusion in the field.  It appeared that one ofthe major contributors to this apparent diversity of results wasthe unstable nature of the subjective data on which studies inthis field have been typically based.
