            Librarians have tried replacing some of their booksand journal files with microfilm copies or other microforms inorder to save valuable space in the bookstacks, instead of or inaddition to extension of the stack area, decentralization, compactshelving, separate storage warehouse, or any of the other solutionsto the storage problem discussed in earlier chapters.  As a finalpaper, this solution for the storage of library materials will bediscussed.  Although the distinct forms will not often be designated,"microform" is used here to mean the four forms most common in theUnited States: 35 mm. roll microfilm, microfiche (now standardized inthe United States and Great Britain at 4 by 6 inches) and the two micro-opaque forms - 3 by 5 inch Microcard, and 6 by 9 inch Microprint.
