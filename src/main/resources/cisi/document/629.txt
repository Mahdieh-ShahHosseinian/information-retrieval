   This article deals with the promotion of information services, specificallythe formation and subsequent evaluation of different promotional programsfor selective dissemination of information (SDI) services provided by theMechanized Information Center (MIC) at the Ohio State University.. Threeprograms -- opinion leadership, "blitz," and telephone solicitation -- were developed.. Data were collected to show, for each of the programs:  (1) the level of market penetration achieved; (2) the level of usersatisfaction generated from the service; (3) the effect in terms ofinfluence, of the various media employed; and (4) cost effectiveness..Data analysis focused on a determination of the most effective methodsto promote SDI services..
