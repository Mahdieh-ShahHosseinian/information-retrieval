This is an interim report on continuingstudies of library operations at MassachusettsInstitute of Technology being made bymembers of the Institute's Operations ResearchProject in cooperation with the librarians andlibrary staff.  In this report, which is basedon actual observations of on-the-premise usemade of the Science Library, various statisticsmeasuring the kind, degree, and intensity of useare tabulated.  In addition there are somesuggested measures of effectiveness of the library.A unique outcome of the survey has been theconstruction and verification of a mathematicalmodel employing probability theory to measurerate and kind of use of library materialtogether with length of stay of library patrons.Such models, characteristic of an operationsresearch approach, give promise of furtheringthe goal of effective library management andplanning.
