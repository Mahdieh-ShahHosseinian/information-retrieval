    Foremost among the nationwide goals of the librarycommunity is the access to needed information resources for allpersons in all locations in the U.S.  The perceived right ofindividuals to such access provides the foundation for nationalinformation planning.    For each library, even the largest, the hope of adequatelysupplying its identified user groups has been greatly constrainedby simultaneous inflation of both cost and quantity of materials.While future technological developments may well make possibleon-line full text retrieval of all library materials at localterminals or the instantaneous remote "publication" of materialsupon demand, the present situation requires more immediatesolutions.
