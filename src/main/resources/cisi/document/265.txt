    The following rules represent a system designed toproduce an instrument essential to the operations and servicesof a library -- its catalog.  To understand the rules and toapply them properly, it is necessary to comprehend the objectives which the catalog is to serve, the method by whichthese objectives are to be achieved, the basic aspects of theproblem of cataloging, and the general principles which underliethe rules.     Objectives.  The objectives which the catalog is to serveare two:     First, to facilitate the location of a particular publication, i.e. of a particular edition of a work, whichis in the library.     Second, to relate and display together the editions whicha library has of a given work and the works which it has of agiven author.
