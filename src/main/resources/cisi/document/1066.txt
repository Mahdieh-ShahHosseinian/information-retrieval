  Although it is hoped that this sourcebook willbe of interest to those of any discipline concernedwith new developments in science of the lastquarter century, it is addressed principally to thestudent of human behavior as that study isapproached from the social side.  This is so partlybecause that is the editor's main competence andinterest, but primarily because the study of humanbehavior is the general area of science that hasleast responded to the exciting challenge of themodern systems outlook.  Yet it is precisely thisgeneral area that stands to gain the most frominsights into the workings of the more complextypes of systems.
