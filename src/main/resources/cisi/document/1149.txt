  This volume is intended for persons concerned with the problems oforganizing human resources and activity.  It is written especially for thosewho are actively engaged in management and supervision and for studentsof administration and organization.  It presents a new theory oforganization based on the management principles and practices of themanagers; who are achieving the best results in American business andgovernment.  It draws also upon research done in voluntary organizations.  To maintain a relatively consistent orientation, the focus of this volumeis largely on the problems of business enterprises.  People interested inother kinds of institutions, such as schools, hospitals, labor unions,professional and voluntary organizations, should, however, experience nodifficulty in applying the general principles of the theory to theirorganizations.
