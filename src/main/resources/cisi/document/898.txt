The intense development of science and its immediate application in productionis one of the characteristic feelings of our time. The growth of the economy now depends more and more on the speed of scientific research.One of the main tasks of the five-year plan (1971-1975) is to develop in everypossible way basic and applied scientific research and to rapidly introduce theirresults into the national economy.The Directives of the 24th Congress of the CPSU emphasize the need to improvescientific-technical information, and to guarantee the systematic transfer tointerested fields and institutions of information on scientific achievementsand progressive experimentation in the areas of technology, the organization ofproduction, and production management.
