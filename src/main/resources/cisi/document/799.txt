   Line's recently proposed technique for correcting the 'apparent' half-lifeto allow for the rate of growth of the literature and Vickery's critical analysis of the proposal are both further analyzed.. Using the concept of utility and considering the sampling variances involved, the paper shows thatLine's technique is both questionable and impractical, and that a further factor - the growth of the number of contributors - needs to be allowed for inVickery's analysis.. A collaborative empirical investigation is proposed..
