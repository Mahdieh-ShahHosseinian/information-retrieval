  The underlying concepts of simulation and experimential teaching methodspresented in this book have been borrowed from other fields - business,governmental and education administration primarily.  The values for libraryeducators are not hypothetical, however, for the methodology has beensuccessfully used over a period of time by a number of teachers, and in avariety of educational settings related to career training for librarians.Neither is the approach overpersonalized.  Much of the telling is in personalterms, in order to limit generalization, to induce acceptance of responsibilityand to provide concrete examples in terms of teaching library administration.The assumption is that it will be easier for library educators to reactcreatively to methodological discourse couched in library science terms thanif the same methodology were described in terms of high finance, internationalpolitics, militarism, or secondary school planning.
