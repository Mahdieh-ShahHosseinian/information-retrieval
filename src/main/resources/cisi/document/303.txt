   One approach to making college library more relevant, dynamic, and intelligible is to employ specialists with broad subject competence, throughlyfamiliar with the terminology, bibliographic tools, and major writings of several related disciplines.. These subject specialists or reference-bibliographers provide reference and institutional services, and serve as coordinators between academic departments and the library.. A significant increase in the quality of library service is attained with reference-bibliographers both building and interpreting the collection.. The reference-bibliographer concept is examined from several perspectives: historicalantecedents; relationship to the academic setting; and the authors' experiencewith a staff of subject specialists at a predominantly undergraduate collegelibrary during 1969-1972..
