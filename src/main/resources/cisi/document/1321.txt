    In reality, the building and shaping of the collection is the heart oflibrarianship, involving the essential philosophy of the profession.  Notonly is it one of the most fascinating tasks in the intellectual world, but"book selection is the most important, most interesting, and most difficultof the professional librarian's responsibilities."  True, ready-made listsof the "best books" need not be ignored, but they have to be evaluatedthoroughly and used only insofar as they prove helpful.
