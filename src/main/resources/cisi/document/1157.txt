Earlier publications on the study of information requests consisted ofstatistical listing of the most usable sources of information.Lately there is an increasing number of publications which consider the methodsfor studying information requests.Analysis of these publications shows that we can discern two basic tendencies:studying subfield groups of researchers and groups of researchers, classifiedby topical information sources.Some attempts were made to study information requests in connection withcreative processes, psychology of creativity and also to establish thepurposes of information retrieval and scientific sources of information.
