  In April 1970, the CAN/SDI project, developedby the Canadian National Science Library,completed its first year of operation.  At thattime the project, designed as a national serviceto alert scientists and technologists to currentinformation in specific fields of research, wasserving over six hundred and fifty subscribersrequiring a total of more than three thousandfive hundred individual computer queries.  Overone thousand personalized bibliographies areprepared each week from commercially availabledata bases purchased from ChemicalAbstracts Service, the Institute for ScientificInformation (ISI) and the British Institution ofElectrical Engineers.  A detailed description ofthe service was given in a paper by J.E.Brown published in October 1969.
