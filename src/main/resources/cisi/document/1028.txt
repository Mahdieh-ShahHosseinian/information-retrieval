  The kind of study presented in this monograph has not as yetwon a wide following among working historians.  On the wholetheir interest has remained with the larger units of socialaction: the region, the class, the party, the ethnic or religiousgroup.  It has been left to the so-called behavioral sciences -anthropology, sociology, psychology - to demonstrate the fundamentalimportance of the smallest and most intimate of all group environments,the family.
