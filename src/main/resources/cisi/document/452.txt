   The U.S. National Library of Medicine (NLM) initiated its computer-based bibliographic information storage and retrieval system, MEDLARS, in 1964..Currently, The NLM has eight international MEDLARS quid-pro-quo arrangements with the United Kingdom, Sweden, France, West Germany, Japan, Australia, Canadaand the World Health Organization.. The policy aspects of the arrangements arediscussed as well as the organizational and operational characteristics of these non-U.S. MEDLARS Centers..
