   Intergroup conflict in libraries is explored, including conflict betweendepartments, between professionals and bureaucracy, and between older and newerstaff members.. Other special interests such as informal power-holders and thesubprofessional are identified.. This analysis shows that existing organizational relationships in libraries let "means" become "ends".. Strongforces toward conformity hamper desirable growth and change.. A restructuringof libraries is proposed along the lines of professional rather than semiprofessional organizations.. Principal changes to be made are in existingprocessing-service relationships and administrative-professional relationships..
